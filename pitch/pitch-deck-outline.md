# Pitch Deck Outline

## Project Title

Community Spirit / Shequ Jingling

Community life RPG and physical-AI social layer for residents, property operators, AI agents, and future service robots.

## Suggested Chinese Title

社区精灵：面向物理 AI 时代的社区生活 RPG 与社会任务层

## 1. Cover

Key message:

Community Spirit turns a residential community into an interactive, maintainable, robot-ready, and socially acceptable task world.

Visual suggestion:

- 2D / 2.5D community map.
- POIs connected by routes.
- Resident, property dashboard, and robot patrol as three entry points.

## 2. Problem

Residential communities are physically close but digitally fragmented.

Resident pain points:

- new residents and visitors get lost;
- service facilities are hard to find;
- repair locations are hard to describe;
- activities are buried in chat groups;
- neighbor connections are low-trust and high-friction.

Property pain points:

- community assets are not operated spatially;
- service workflows are form-based and hard to visualize;
- activity participation is hard to manage;
- digital twin projects often stop at display;
- future robots lack structured community maps.

## 3. Why Now

Market timing:

- property management is moving from expansion to operational efficiency;
- digital twin technology is becoming cheaper and more accessible;
- AI agents need structured real-world context;
- service robots need semantic maps and task interfaces;
- residents expect more convenient and visual service experiences.

Key message:

The next community platform should not be only an app. It should be a spatial operating layer.

## 4. Solution

Community Spirit provides:

- lightweight community digital twin;
- POI-based spatial data;
- resident and visitor routes;
- activity signup and check-in;
- repair reporting with location context;
- property operation dashboard;
- AI community assistant;
- robot-ready semantic route export.

Core formula:

```text
Space + Task + Activity + AI + Robot-ready Semantics
```

## 5. Demo Scenario

Fictional demo community:

OmniWeave Community / Wanlian Chuangjie

Demo tasks:

## 8. Competition and Differentiation

Important framing:

Community Spirit should not claim that nobody has built smart community, AI property, digital twin, or robot-operation products. Those categories already exist.

The differentiation is the product shape: a resident-visible, property-useful, robot-ready spatial task layer.

Nearby categories:

- property management apps: workflow-first, not spatial-first;
- digital twin vendors: often display-first or dashboard-first;
- resident service apps: service-first, not map/task-first;
- AI property copilots: maintenance and asset-first;
- robot companies: fleet and device-first, not community semantic layer-first.

Differentiation:

```text
Resident-facing map
+ daily spatial tasks
+ property dashboard
+ AI intent matching
+ robot-ready semantic export
```

Defensible line:

Community Spirit connects existing smart-community directions through a lightweight POI / Route / Task / Activity model.
